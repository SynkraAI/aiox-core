# Offer Quality Checklist

Quality gate for offers built with `*build-offer` command. Based on Hormozi Value Equation + Grand Slam methodology.

**Purpose:** Ensure every offer maximizes perceived value and minimizes perceived cost/risk.

---

## Value Equation (Hormozi)

### Dream Outcome
- [ ] Dream outcome clearly defined (what they get, not how)
- [ ] Outcome is specific and measurable
- [ ] Dream Outcome score: ≥8/10 (rate the desirability)
- [ ] Outcome addresses the #1 problem for the avatar

### Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
- [ ] Proof elements present (testimonials, case studies, data)
- [ ] At least 3 social proof items with specific metrics
- [ ] Authority/credibility established (who you are, why you can deliver)
- [ ] Perceived Likelihood score: ≥8/10 (prospect believes they'll get the result)

### Time Delay
- [ ] Time to result is clearly stated
- [ ] Time Delay is ≤3 months (or justified if longer)
- [ ] Quick wins identified (results within first 7-14 days)
- [ ] Time Delay score: ≤3/10 (lower is better)

### Effort & Sacrifice
- [ ] Effort required is clearly communicated
- [ ] Offer reduces effort via "done-for-you" or simplified process
- [ ] Effort & Sacrifice score: ≤3/10 (lower is better)
- [ ] Objection handled: "This is different from what I tried before"

---

## Offer Stack (Brunson/Hormozi)

- [ ] Core offer clearly defined (main deliverable)
- [ ] At least 3 bonuses included
- [ ] Each bonus solves a related problem or speeds up results
- [ ] Each bonus has a named value (e.g., "$997 value")
- [ ] Total perceived value ≥10x the actual price
- [ ] Stack is presented in order of increasing value
- [ ] Each element has a "what" and a "why" (benefit)

---

## Risk Reversal (Guarantee)

- [ ] Guarantee is present
- [ ] Guarantee type: Unconditional OR Conditional (mark which)
- [ ] Guarantee duration stated (30/60/90 days or "forever")
- [ ] Guarantee is better than standard (e.g., double money back, results guarantee)
- [ ] Guarantee shifts risk from prospect to seller
- [ ] Guarantee language is clear and believable

---

## Scarcity & Urgency

### Scarcity (Real Limitation)
- [ ] Scarcity mechanism present
- [ ] Scarcity is REAL (limited seats, time, bonuses, or access)
- [ ] Scarcity type: Cohort-based / Capacity / Bonus / Seasonal (mark which)
- [ ] Scarcity is communicated clearly

### Urgency (Deadline)
- [ ] Deadline present
- [ ] Deadline is tied to real event (cohort start, bonus expiration, price increase)
- [ ] Urgency is ethical (not manufactured)
- [ ] Countdown or expiration date clearly stated

---

## Positioning & Naming

- [ ] Offer name communicates dream outcome, not mechanism
- [ ] Offer creates a "category of one" (incomparable to alternatives)
- [ ] Positioning statement clearly defines who it's for and who it's NOT for
- [ ] Offer disqualifies the wrong people (increases desire for right people)
- [ ] Offer avoids commoditization (not "coaching" or "consulting")

---

## Pricing & Anchoring

- [ ] Price is clearly stated
- [ ] Price is anchored against higher value (value stack first, then price)
- [ ] Payment plans offered (if applicable)
- [ ] Price is congruent with positioning (premium price = premium positioning)
- [ ] Price objection is pre-handled in copy/script

---

## Proof & Credibility

- [ ] At least 3 testimonials or case studies present
- [ ] Testimonials include specific metrics (e.g., "$50K in 30 days")
- [ ] Testimonials address common objections ("I was skeptical too...")
- [ ] Before/after transformations shown
- [ ] Authority markers present (credentials, media, results)

---

## Call-to-Action (CTA)

- [ ] CTA is clear and singular (one next step)
- [ ] CTA is low-friction (application, call booking, checkout)
- [ ] CTA is repeated at least 3 times in presentation
- [ ] CTA urgency reinforced ("Limited spots", "Closes Friday")
