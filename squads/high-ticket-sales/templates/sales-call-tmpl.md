Complete sales call script following SPIN Selling (Rackham) + Challenger Sale (Dixon/Adamson) + Frame Control (Klaff) methodologies.

## Pre-Call Prep (Before the Call)

### Research Prospect
- [ ] Review application/intake form
- [ ] Check LinkedIn profile
- [ ] Review website/social media (if applicable)
- [ ] Identify 2-3 specific pains/goals from their context
- [ ] Prepare personalized examples/stories

### Set Intention
- [ ] I am the prize (frame control)
- [ ] I'm here to help, not to sell
- [ ] Disqualify if not a fit
- [ ] Control the agenda

### Environment
- [ ] Quiet space, no distractions
- [ ] Water nearby
- [ ] Screen share ready (if needed)
- [ ] Notes template open

## Opening / Frame Setting (3-5 minutes)

### Greeting + Rapport
"Hey [NAME], great to meet you! Thanks for taking the time today."

[Small talk — 30-60 seconds max. Reference something from their application/profile.]

### Agenda Setting (Control the Frame)
"So here's how I like to run these calls:

First, I'm going to ask you a few questions to understand where you are, where you want to go, and what's been stopping you.

Then, if it makes sense, I'll share how [OFFER NAME] works and whether it's a fit for you.

And if it's not a fit, I'll be the first to tell you. Sound good?"

[Wait for agreement. They just agreed to YOUR agenda.]

### Status Reframe (You Are the Prize)
"Before we dive in, I want to be upfront: [OFFER NAME] isn't for everyone. We only work with [SPECIFIC AVATAR TRAIT], and we're pretty selective about who we bring on.

So I'm going to ask some tough questions to see if this is even a fit. Fair enough?"

[Wait for agreement. You've now positioned yourself as the authority.]

---

## SPIN Discovery Questions (15-20 minutes)

### Situation Questions (2-3 questions max)
**Goal:** Understand current state without interrogating.

"Tell me a bit about your business/situation right now."

"How long have you been [DOING X]?"

"What does your current [PROCESS/SYSTEM] look like?"

[Listen. Take notes. Don't jump to solutions yet.]

---

### Problem Questions (5-7 questions)
**Goal:** Identify the pain and its impact.

"What's the biggest challenge you're facing with [AREA]?"

"What's frustrating you most about [CURRENT SITUATION]?"

"How is [PROBLEM] affecting your [BUSINESS/LIFE/REVENUE]?"

"If you had to put a number on it, what's [PROBLEM] costing you per month?"

[DIG DEEPER. Get them to articulate the pain in their own words.]

---

### Implication Questions (3-5 questions)
**Goal:** Amplify the pain by exploring consequences of inaction.

"If you don't solve [PROBLEM], where do you see yourself 6 months from now?"

"What happens to your [BUSINESS/FAMILY/GOALS] if this continues?"

"Have you thought about the opportunity cost of staying stuck here?"

"What's the worst-case scenario if nothing changes?"

[Let silence do the heavy lifting. They need to FEEL the pain.]

---

### Need-Payoff Questions (3-5 questions)
**Goal:** Get them to articulate the value of solving the problem BEFORE you pitch.

"If you could solve [PROBLEM], what would that mean for your business?"

"How would your life be different if [PROBLEM] was gone?"

"What would it be worth to you to [ACHIEVE DESIRED OUTCOME]?"

"If I could show you how to [SOLVE PROBLEM] in [TIMEFRAME], would that be valuable?"

[They're now selling themselves. When they say "yes," you've won.]

---

## Teaching / Reframe Moment (5-7 minutes)

### Challenger Insight
"Okay, so based on what you've told me, I want to share something that might shift your perspective..."

**Reframe Statement:**
"The reason you're struggling with [PROBLEM] isn't because [COMMON BELIEF]. It's actually because [NEW INSIGHT]."

**Example:** "Most people think the reason they can't close high-ticket deals is because they don't have enough leads. But the real issue is they're using low-ticket tactics for high-ticket offers."

### Teach the New Way
"Here's what's actually happening..."

[Share your unique mechanism/framework. Use a whiteboard, screen share, or story. Keep it 3-5 minutes max.]

### Aha Moment Check
"Does that make sense? Have you noticed this in your own business?"

[Wait for acknowledgment. They need to agree with the insight before you pitch.]

---

## Solution Presentation (8-10 minutes)

### Transition
"So based on everything you've shared, here's how [OFFER NAME] works..."

### Map Offer to Their Pains
**Don't just list features. Tie each component to THEIR specific pains.**

"You mentioned [PAIN 1]. Inside [OFFER], we solve that with [COMPONENT 1]. Here's how..."

"You also said [PAIN 2]. That's why we include [COMPONENT 2], which [BENEFIT]..."

"And the third thing you mentioned was [PAIN 3]. We handle that through [COMPONENT 3]..."

### Social Proof (Relevant to Them)
"I had a client, [NAME], who was in a similar situation. They were [SIMILAR PAIN]. After [TIMEFRAME], they [RESULT]. Here's what they said..."

[Share 1-2 testimonials/case studies that mirror the prospect's situation.]

### Value Stack (Brief)
"So just to recap, here's everything you get:
- [Core Deliverable 1]
- [Core Deliverable 2]
- [Core Deliverable 3]
- [Bonus 1]
- [Bonus 2]
- [Bonus 3]"

[Don't dwell on this. You've already built the value through discovery.]

## Objection Handling Scripts (As Needed)

### Price Objection: "It's too expensive."

**Label + Reframe:**
"I hear you. It's a significant investment. Let me ask you this: Earlier you said [PROBLEM] is costing you $[AMOUNT] per month. That's $[ANNUAL COST] per year. This is [PRICE], and based on [CLIENT RESULTS], most clients make that back in [TIMEFRAME]. Does that change the math?"

**Alternative:**
"Too expensive compared to what? Staying stuck? Or compared to the $[AMOUNT] you said you'd make if you solved this?"

---

### Time Objection: "I don't have time right now."

**Label + Reframe:**
"I totally get that. You're busy. But let me ask: If you had [RESULT], would that actually SAVE you time? Because most of our clients find that [OFFER] actually buys them time back."

**Implication:**
"And if you wait, where will you be 3 months from now? Still saying you don't have time?"

---

### Spouse Objection: "I need to talk to my husband/wife/partner."

**Pre-Frame (Prevent This):**
"Before we get started, is there anyone else who needs to be part of this decision? If so, let's get them on the call."

**If It Comes Up:**
"Absolutely, I respect that. What do you think their main concern will be?"

[Uncover the REAL objection. It's not the spouse. It's something else.]

**Alternative:**
"If your partner was here right now, what would you tell them about why this makes sense?"

---

### Skepticism Objection: "I've tried this before and it didn't work."

**Label + Differentiate:**
"I hear you. And that makes sense. What specifically did you try before?"

[Listen. Then differentiate.]

"Okay, so the reason that didn't work is [REASON]. What we do differently is [NEW MECHANISM]. That's why [CLIENT EXAMPLE] got [RESULT] even though they'd tried [OLD METHOD] before."

---

### Timing Objection: "Can I think about it and get back to you?"

**Soft Challenge:**
"Absolutely. Let me ask though: What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the price, the time commitment, or something else?"

[Uncover the real objection. Then handle it.]

**Urgency Reinforcement:**
"And just so you know, we only have [NUMBER] spots this month, and we're [PERCENTAGE] full. If you decide later, we might not have space."

---

## Close Technique (3-5 minutes)

### Trial Close (Before Final Ask)
"So at this point, does what I've shared make sense? Do you see how this could help you [ACHIEVE GOAL]?"

[If yes, proceed. If no, uncover objection.]

---

### Assumptive Close
"Great. So here's what happens next..."

[Don't ask "Do you want to move forward?" Assume they do.]

"We'll get you set up today. We have [PAYMENT OPTIONS]. Which works better for you: pay in full or payment plan?"

---

### Binary Choice Close (Klaff)
"So we have two options:
1. [OPTION A — Full price, best bonuses]
2. [OPTION B — Payment plan, standard bonuses]

Which one feels like a better fit?"

[You're not asking "yes or no." You're asking "which one."]

---

### Emotional + Logical Close
**Emotional:**
"Think about where you'll be 90 days from now if you do this. [PAINT PICTURE]. That's what's possible."

**Logical:**
"And with the [GUARANTEE], you're protected. The only risk is doing nothing and staying stuck."

---

### Silence Close
[After you ask for the sale, SHUT UP. The first person to speak loses.]

[Wait 5-10 seconds. Let them process.]

---

## Final Decision Points

### If They Say YES
"Amazing. Let me get you set up right now."

[Process payment or send contract immediately. Strike while iron is hot.]

"Welcome to the program! Here's what happens next..."

---

### If They're Still Hesitant
"I hear you. Let me ask: On a scale of 1-10, how interested are you?"

[If 7+:] "What would it take to make this a 10?"

[If <7:] "What's holding you back?"

[Uncover and handle final objection.]

---

### If They Say NO (But Qualified)
"No problem. I appreciate you being honest. Out of curiosity, what's the main reason?"

[Uncover objection for future reference.]

"Totally fair. If anything changes or you have questions down the road, feel free to reach out."

[Don't chase. Let them come back to you.]

## Post-Call Follow-Up Sequence

### Call Summary Email (Within 1 Hour)
**Subject:** "Great talking to you, [NAME]"

**Body:**
"Hey [NAME],

Really enjoyed our conversation today. Here's a quick recap:

- You're struggling with [PAIN 1, PAIN 2, PAIN 3]
- You want to [GOAL]
- [OFFER NAME] solves this through [MECHANISM]

[If they said yes:] Next steps: [ONBOARDING]

[If they're thinking:] Here's the link to get started when you're ready: [LINK]. Remember, we only have [NUMBER] spots left this month.

Let me know if you have any questions!

[YOUR NAME]"

---

### Follow-Up Touch 2 (T+24h if thinking)
**Subject:** "[NAME], quick question"

**Body:**
"Hey [NAME],

I know you're thinking things over. I just wanted to check in — do you have any questions I can answer?

Also, just a heads up: We only have [NUMBER] spots left, so if you want to move forward, let me know soon.

[YOUR NAME]"

---

### Follow-Up Touch 3 (T+48h if thinking)
**Subject:** "Thought of you..."

**Body:**
"Hey [NAME],

I just got off the phone with [CLIENT NAME], who was in a similar spot as you 90 days ago. They just hit [RESULT].

Made me think of you. If you're ready to move forward, here's the link: [LINK].

[YOUR NAME]"

---

### Follow-Up Touch 4 (T+72h if thinking)
**Subject:** "Last call"

**Body:**
"Hey [NAME],

Just wanted to give you a heads up: We're closing enrollment on [DATE], and after that, we won't be opening again until [FUTURE DATE].

If you want in, now's the time. Here's the link: [LINK].

If not, no worries — just wanted to make sure you didn't miss the window.

[YOUR NAME]"

---

### Follow-Up Touch 5 (T+7 days if no response)
**Subject:** "Checking in"

**Body:**
"Hey [NAME],

Haven't heard from you in a bit. Just wanted to check in — are you still interested in [SOLVING PROBLEM]?

If so, let's hop on a quick call and figure out next steps.

If not, no worries. I'll take you off my follow-up list.

Let me know!

[YOUR NAME]"
