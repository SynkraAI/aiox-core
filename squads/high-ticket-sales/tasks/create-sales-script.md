## Framework Reference

**Framework 3: Consultative Sales Call System**
- SPIN Selling: Situation → Problem → Implication → Need-Payoff
- Challenger Sale: Teach → Tailor → Take Control
- Chris Voss: Tactical empathy + calibrated questions
- Zig Ziglar: Help them get what they want
- CLOSER Framework: Clarify → Label → Overview → Sell → Explain → Reinforce

## Checklist

### Step 1: Rapport & Frame (2-3 min)

- [ ] Open with pattern interrupt: "Thanks for making time. This call is different from what you might expect..."
- [ ] Set agenda: "Here's how this will work: I'll ask questions for X minutes, then I'll share what I think makes sense for you"
- [ ] Establish authority: "I've worked with X people in your situation..."
- [ ] Disarm resistance: "At the end, if this isn't a fit, I'll tell you. And if it is a fit but not right for you, you can tell me. Sound fair?"
- [ ] Create safety: "I'm not here to convince you of anything. I'm here to see if we can help"
- [ ] Set frame: You're the doctor diagnosing, they're the patient sharing symptoms

**Frame Control Principles:**
- Expert frame (you diagnose, they respond)
- Collaborative frame (we're exploring together)
- Permission frame (they invited you to assess)

### Step 2: SPIN Discovery (10-15 min)

**Situation Questions (2-3 min):**
- [ ] "Walk me through your current situation with [problem area]..."
- [ ] "How long have you been dealing with this?"
- [ ] "What have you tried so far?"
- [ ] "What's your current [revenue/process/metric]?"

**Problem Questions (3-4 min):**
- [ ] "What's the biggest challenge you're facing with [situation]?"
- [ ] "What's frustrating about [current approach]?"
- [ ] "Where do you get stuck?"
- [ ] "What keeps you up at night about this?"

**Implication Questions (3-4 min):**
- [ ] "What does this cost you? Not just money — time, stress, opportunity?"
- [ ] "If this doesn't change in the next 6-12 months, where will you be?"
- [ ] "How does this affect [other areas of life/business]?"
- [ ] "What are you missing out on because of this?"

**Need-Payoff Questions (2-3 min):**
- [ ] "If you could solve this, what would that mean to you?"
- [ ] "What would be possible if [problem] was gone?"
- [ ] "How would your life/business be different?"
- [ ] "What's the value of solving this? Not just financially — personally?"

**Discovery Success Criteria:**
- They're doing 80% of the talking
- They've identified their own pain (not you telling them)
- They've articulated the cost of inaction
- They've painted the picture of success

### Step 3: Challenge & Reframe (5-7 min)

**Identify the Pattern:**
- [ ] "Based on what you shared, here's what I'm hearing... [reflect back]"
- [ ] "Can I share an observation?" [Get permission]
- [ ] "I've seen this pattern before with [X clients]..."

**Challenge Current Thinking:**
- [ ] "The reason [current approach] isn't working is because [insight]..."
- [ ] "Most people think [false belief], but what I've found is [truth]..."
- [ ] "You're not the problem. The strategy is the problem."
- [ ] "Let me show you what's actually happening here..." [Reframe]

**Teach the New Way:**
- [ ] "Here's what needs to happen instead..." [Introduce vehicle]
- [ ] "The people who succeed do [X] instead of [Y]"
- [ ] "This is why [your method] works when [old way] doesn't..."

**Challenger Sale Pattern:**
1. Teach: "Here's what you don't know about your situation"
2. Tailor: "Here's what this means for you specifically"
3. Take Control: "Here's what needs to happen"

### Step 4: Solution Presentation (5-7 min)

**Position the Offer:**
- [ ] "Based on everything you've shared, here's what I recommend..."
- [ ] "We have a [offer name] designed specifically for [their situation]"

**Present the Solution:**
- [ ] Core deliverable: "Here's what you get... [be specific]"
- [ ] Implementation: "Here's how it works... [process]"
- [ ] Support: "Here's how we support you... [touchpoints]"
- [ ] Timeline: "Here's what happens in the first 30/60/90 days..."

**Tie Back to Their Words:**
- [ ] "Remember you said [their problem]? This solves that by [solution]"
- [ ] "You mentioned wanting [their goal]? This gets you there through [method]"
- [ ] "This directly addresses [their pain point #1, #2, #3]"

**Present Investment:**
- [ ] "The investment for this is [price]"
- [ ] Add guarantee: "And you're protected by [guarantee details]"
- [ ] Create urgency: "We only take on [X] clients per month" (if true)

### Step 5: Objection Handling (5-10 min)

**Common Objections & Responses:**

**"I need to think about it"**
- [ ] "Absolutely. What specifically do you need to think about?"
- [ ] [Listen] "Got it. On a scale of 1-10, how important is solving [problem]?"
- [ ] "And how confident are you that [current path] will get you there?"
- [ ] "What would need to be true for this to be a no-brainer?"

**"I need to talk to my [spouse/partner]"**
- [ ] "Of course. What do you think they'll say?"
- [ ] "What questions will they have that I can help you answer now?"
- [ ] "If they said yes, would you be ready to move forward?"
- [ ] "Would it help if we got them on a quick call so I can answer their questions directly?"

**"I can't afford it"**
- [ ] "I understand. Can I ask — if money wasn't an issue, would this be exactly what you need?"
- [ ] "How much is [problem] costing you per month? How does that compare to [price/month]?"
- [ ] "Is it that you can't afford it, or that you don't see the value?"
- [ ] "What if we could make the payment work? Would you be interested in exploring options?"

**"I need to try on my own first"**
- [ ] "I respect that. How long have you been trying on your own?"
- [ ] "What would be different this time?"
- [ ] "If you could get there on your own, you probably would have by now. What's missing?"
- [ ] "How much longer are you willing to stay stuck before getting help?"

**"I'm already working with someone else"**
- [ ] "Got it. How's that going for you?"
- [ ] "Are you getting the results you want?"
- [ ] "What would you need to see to know it's time to try something different?"
- [ ] "It sounds like if they were going to solve this, they would have by now. Fair?"

**Objection Handling Principles (Voss):**
- Label emotions: "It sounds like you're [concerned/hesitant/uncertain]..."
- Calibrated questions: "How am I supposed to do that?" (makes them problem-solve)
- Mirroring: Repeat last 3 words to get them talking

### Step 6: Close Sequence (2-3 min)

**Trial Close:**
- [ ] "Based on everything we've talked about, does this feel like the right solution for you?"

**If YES:**
- [ ] "Great. Let's get you started. Here's what happens next..."
- [ ] Walk through onboarding process
- [ ] Handle payment/paperwork
- [ ] Set expectations: "You'll receive [X] within [timeframe]"
- [ ] Book first call/session
- [ ] End with certainty: "You made the right decision. I'll see you [when]"

**If HESITATION:**
- [ ] "What's holding you back?"
- [ ] [Listen, label, address]
- [ ] "On a scale of 1-10, where are you?"
- [ ] "What would get you to a 10?"
- [ ] [Address gap, close again]

**If NO:**
- [ ] "No problem. Can I ask what specifically doesn't feel right?"
- [ ] [Diagnose: Wrong offer? Wrong time? Wrong fit?]
- [ ] "Is this a 'no' or a 'not right now'?"
- [ ] "What would need to change for this to be a yes?"
- [ ] [If truly not a fit] "I appreciate your honesty. Let me know if anything changes"

**Close Principles (Ziglar):**
- Assume the sale (not "if" but "when")
- Make it easy (remove friction)
- End with action (next step is clear)

### Step 7: Quality Check & Refinement (15-20 min)

- [ ] Review entire script for natural flow
- [ ] Check question quality: Are they open-ended? Do they create urgency?
- [ ] Validate objection responses: Do they address root concerns?
- [ ] Test conversational tone: Does this sound like you?
- [ ] Verify timing: Does each section fit the call duration?
- [ ] Role-play with colleague: Does it feel authentic?
- [ ] Run through `checklists/sales-call-checklist.md` (if exists)

## Execution Notes

**Duration:** 3-4 hours for complete call script

**Call Duration Adaptations:**

| Duration | Adjustments |
|----------|-------------|
| **30 min** | Compress discovery (7-8 min), quick presentation (3-4 min) |
| **45 min** | Standard structure as outlined |
| **60 min** | Extended discovery (15-20 min), detailed presentation (10 min) |

**Iteration Pattern:**
1. First pass: Get framework and key questions on paper
2. Second pass: Refine objection handling with real scenarios
3. Third pass: Practice aloud and adjust for natural flow

**Common Pitfalls:**
- Talking too much (should be 70/30 them/you in discovery)
- Weak questions (closed-ended, no implication)
- Generic objection handling (not tied to their words)
- Pushy close (destroys rapport)
- No frame (sounds like begging)

**Success Criteria:**
- Discovery reveals self-diagnosed pain
- Challenge creates aha moment
- Solution feels tailored (not generic pitch)
- Objections addressed without defensiveness
- Close feels natural (not forced)
