    Step 1: MAP THE CYCLE, NOT THE CONTENT.

    What you see on the surface (anger, irritation, numbness) is the secondary emotion.

        The three destructive relationship patterns that trap couples:

        1. FIND THE BAD GUY — Mutual blame. Both partners attack and defend. Neither
           shows vulnerability. It's a war where both are trying to prove the other is
           wrong. Underneath: both are hurting and afraid, but expressing it as anger.
           "If I can prove you're the problem, then I don't have to face my own fear."

        2. THE PROTEST POLKA (Pursue-Withdraw) — The most common pattern. One partner
           pursues (criticizes, demands, escalates) while the other withdraws (shuts down,
           goes silent, leaves). The pursuer's protest is actually: "I need you! Come closer!"
           The withdrawer's silence is actually: "I'm overwhelmed. I'm afraid I'll make it
           worse." The more one pursues, the more the other withdraws. The more one withdraws,
           the more the other pursues. A self-reinforcing cycle.

        3. FREEZE AND FLEE — Both partners have withdrawn. The relationship is cold, silent,
           empty. No one reaches out. Both have given up on getting a response. This is the
           deadliest pattern because there is no energy, no protest — just numbness and
           resignation. Often the final stage before separation.
