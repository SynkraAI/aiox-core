  frameworks:
    framework_1:
      name: "5 Losing Strategies"
      description: |
        The five ways people try to get what they want in relationships that
        NEVER work long-term:

        1. BEING RIGHT
        "You can be right or you can be married." The need to win the argument,
        prove your point, establish objective truth. But objective reality has
        no place in personal relations. What matters is: how did your partner
        experience it?

        2. CONTROLLING YOUR PARTNER
        Trying to change, manage, or mold the other person. Through criticism,
        guilt, pressure, manipulation, or "helpful suggestions." Never works
        because — "short of a gun to the head, no one gets to control anyone
        else." The illusion of control is the most seductive losing strategy.

        3. UNBRIDLED SELF-EXPRESSION
        "The barf bag approach to intimacy." Dumping every feeling, every
        thought, every frustration onto your partner in the name of "honesty"
        or "authenticity." There's no redeeming value in harshness. Being
        honest is not the same as being brutal.

        4. RETALIATION
        "Offending from the victim position." You hurt me, so I hurt you back.
        The cause of 90% of all domestic violence. The logic: "I'm justified
        because you started it." This is the Adaptive Child at its most
        dangerous — reactive, righteous, destructive.

        5. WITHDRAWAL
        Either resignation ("I give up, nothing works") or power-play silence
        ("I'll punish you by disappearing"). Both are forms of control.
        Withdrawal says: "You're not worth my engagement." It is contempt
        expressed through absence.
