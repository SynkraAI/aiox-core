## 1. Alex Hormozi - $100M Offers Framework

### 1.1 The Value Equation

```
                Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
Value = ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
              Time Delay × Effort and Sacrifice

To INCREASE value:
├── INCREASE Dream Outcome (make the result bigger)
├── INCREASE Perceived Likelihood (make them believe it will work)
├── DECREASE Time Delay (make it faster)
└── DECREASE Effort & Sacrifice (make it easier)
```

#### Applying the Value Equation

| Lever | Strategy | Example |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **Dream Outcome ↑** | Paint vivid picture of transformation | "Go from $5k/mo to $50k/mo" not "Grow your business" |
| **Likelihood ↑** | Social proof, guarantees, track record | Case studies, testimonials, risk reversal |
| **Time Delay ↓** | Quick wins, fast onboarding, instant access | "See results in 7 days" not "Eventually you'll see..." |
| **Effort ↓** | Done-for-you, templates, automation | "We handle everything" vs "Here's a course" |

### 1.2 The Grand Slam Offer

#### Step 1: Identify the Dream Outcome
```
Questions to ask:
- What is the #1 thing your ideal customer wants?
- What would their life look like if they achieved it?
- What would they pay to have this problem solved forever?
- What's the emotional state they're trying to reach?
```

#### Step 2: List All Problems (The Problem List)
```
For the dream outcome, list EVERY problem the customer faces:

Before purchasing:
- "I don't know if this will work for me"
- "I can't afford it"
- "I don't have time"

During usage:
- "I don't know what to do first"
- "I get stuck and need help"
- "I can't stay motivated"

After achieving result:
- "How do I maintain this?"
- "What do I do next?"
- "How do I scale?"
```

#### Step 3: Create Solutions for Every Problem
```
For each problem → Create a solution
For each solution → Name it, package it, assign value

Problem: "I don't know what to do first"
Solution: "The Quick-Start Blueprint" (step-by-step 7-day plan)
Value: Worth $497 alone

Problem: "I get stuck and need help"
Solution: "Direct Access Hotline" (24/7 Slack support)
Value: Worth $997/month
```

#### Step 4: The Delivery Cube

```
             ┌─────────────────────────────────┐
             │     DELIVERY CUBE               │
             │                                 │
             │  1:1  ←──────→  1:Many          │
             │   ↑                   ↑         │
             │   │   DFY    DIY     │         │
             │   │    ↑      ↑      │         │
             │   │    │      │      │         │
             │   ↓                   ↓         │
             │  High ←──────→  Low             │
             │  Touch           Touch          │
             └─────────────────────────────────┘

Delivery Methods (from highest to lowest value):
1. Done-For-You (DFY) - Highest price, highest value
2. Done-With-You (DWY) - High price, high engagement
3. Do-It-Yourself (DIY) - Lower price, scalable
```

#### Step 5: Trim and Stack

```
Grand Slam Offer Stack:

Core Offer: [Main Product/Service]
├── Bonus 1: [Solves Problem A] - Value $X
├── Bonus 2: [Solves Problem B] - Value $Y
```

### 1.3 Pricing Principles (Hormozi)

```
Rules:
1. Price based on VALUE delivered, not cost to deliver
2. Price should make customers THINK, not say "that's cheap"
3. Higher prices = more committed customers = better results
4. Higher prices = more margin = better service = more investment
5. If no one complains about price, it's too LOW

Price Anchoring:
"The total value of everything you get is $24,000.
But you're not going to pay $24,000.
You're not even going to pay $12,000.
Your investment today is just $4,997."
```

### 1.4 Guarantees (Risk Reversal)

| Type | Description | Risk Level | Example |
|------|-------------|------------|---------|
| **Unconditional** | Full refund, no questions | High (for seller) | "30-day money-back guarantee" |
| **Conditional** | Refund if specific criteria met | Medium | "If you do X, Y, Z and don't get results, full refund" |
| **Anti-guarantee** | No refund, all sales final | Low (for seller) | "We're so confident, we don't offer refunds" |
| **Performance** | Results-based guarantee | Very High | "We'll work free until you hit $10k/mo" |
| **Implied** | Industry reputation | None | Premium brand positioning |

## 2. Chris Voss - Negotiation Mastery

### 2.1 Tactical Empathy

```
Tactical Empathy is NOT:
- Agreeing with the other side
- Being nice or weak
- Giving in to demands

Tactical Empathy IS:
- Understanding the other side's perspective
- Demonstrating that understanding
- Using that understanding strategically
```

### 2.2 Core Techniques

#### Mirroring
```
Technique: Repeat the last 1-3 words (or critical words) as a question.

Prospect: "We've been having issues with our current provider."
You: "Issues with your current provider?"
Prospect: [Elaborates extensively]

Why it works:
- Triggers elaboration
- Makes people feel heard
- Buys you thinking time
- Creates rapport unconsciously
```

#### Labeling
```
Technique: Name the emotion you observe.
Format: "It seems like..." / "It sounds like..." / "It looks like..."

"It seems like you're frustrated with the lack of support."
"It sounds like reliability is really important to your team."
"It looks like you've been burned before by vendors who over-promised."

Rules:
- Never use "I" statements ("I think you feel...")
- Use third-person labels ("It seems...")
- Pause after labeling (let them confirm/deny)
- Negative labels defuse; positive labels reinforce
```

#### Calibrated Questions
```
Start with "How" and "What" - never "Why" (feels accusatory)

Discovery:
- "What's the biggest challenge you're facing right now?"
- "How does this problem affect your team's day-to-day?"
- "What happens if you don't solve this in the next 6 months?"

Negotiation:
- "How am I supposed to do that?" (pushes back without saying no)
- "What makes this important to you?"
- "How would you like me to proceed?"

Closing:
- "What's stopping us from moving forward?"
- "How can we make this work for both of us?"
- "What does a successful partnership look like to you?"
```

#### The Accusation Audit
```
Pre-empt every negative thing they might think/say about you:

"You're probably thinking this is too expensive."
"You might feel like we're just another vendor."
"You may think we're too small to handle your scale."
"I'm sure you're wondering why you should trust us."

Then address each one.

Why it works:
- Defuses negativity before it forms
- Shows self-awareness and confidence
- Prospect often responds "It's not THAT bad..."
```

### 2.3 The Black Swan Theory

```
Black Swans = Unknown unknowns that change everything

Finding Black Swans:
1. Ask open-ended questions
2. Listen for what's NOT being said
3. Watch for emotional shifts
4. Explore throwaway comments
5. Meet face-to-face when possible

Types of Leverage:
├── Positive: "I can give you what you want"
├── Negative: "I can hurt you if you don't deal"
└── Normative: "Your own standards/values support my position"
```

### 2.4 The "No" Framework

```
"No" is the start of negotiation, not the end.

Types of "No":
1. "I'm not yet ready to agree" → Need more info
2. "You're making me uncomfortable" → Back off, re-approach
3. "I don't understand" → Clarify the offer
4. "I can't afford it" → Restructure the deal
5. "I want something else" → Discover what they really want

Getting to "No" (counterintuitive):
- "Is it a bad idea to...?" (Gets them saying no, which feels safe)
- "Have you given up on...?" (Triggers "No, I haven't given up!")
- "Would it be unreasonable to...?" (No = agreement)
```

### 2.5 The Ackerman Model (Bargaining)

```
Step 1: Set target price (what you actually want)
Step 2: Set opening offer at 65% of target
Step 3: Plan three raises: 85%, 95%, 100% of target
Step 4: Use empathy and labels between each raise
Step 5: Final number should be non-round ($97,350 not $97,000)
Step 6: Include a non-monetary item at final offer

Example (Target: $100,000):
Round 1: $65,000 + empathy
Round 2: $85,000 + label their frustration
Round 3: $95,000 + calibrated question
Round 4: $97,350 + "I'll throw in 6 months of support"
```

## 3. Sales Methodologies

### 3.1 SPIN Selling (Neil Rackham)

```
S - Situation Questions (Gather facts)
    "How many users do you currently have?"
    "What tools are you using today?"
    "How is your team structured?"

P - Problem Questions (Identify pains)
    "What challenges do you face with onboarding?"
    "Where do you see bottlenecks?"
    "What takes the most time in your current process?"

I - Implication Questions (Amplify the pain)
    "How does that bottleneck affect your quarterly targets?"
    "What happens to customer satisfaction when onboarding is slow?"
    "If this problem continues for 12 months, what's the cost?"

N - Need-Payoff Questions (Show the value)
    "How would it help if onboarding took 2 days instead of 2 weeks?"
    "What would it mean for your team to eliminate that bottleneck?"
    "If you could reclaim those hours, where would you invest them?"
```

#### SPIN Flow

```
Situation → Problem → Implication → Need-Payoff → Present Solution
    ↑                                                      │
    └──────────── If objection, return to SPIN ────────────┘
```

### 3.2 The Challenger Sale

#### Five Sales Rep Profiles

| Profile | Characteristics | Win Rate |
|---------|----------------|----------|
| **Challenger** | Teaches, tailors, takes control | 40% of top performers |
| **Lone Wolf** | Self-assured, follows own instincts | 25% |
| **Hard Worker** | Diligent, always going the extra mile | 17% |
| **Relationship Builder** | Likeable, generous, gets along | 11% |
| **Problem Solver** | Reliable, detail-oriented, responsive | 7% |

#### The Challenger Approach

```
1. TEACH - Provide unique insights the customer hasn't considered
   "Did you know that 73% of companies like yours lose $X annually to..."

2. TAILOR - Customize the message to the stakeholder
   CFO → ROI, cost savings, risk reduction
   CTO → Architecture, scalability, integration
   CEO → Strategic advantage, market position

3. TAKE CONTROL - Guide the sale, push back constructively
   "Based on what I've seen with similar companies, I'd recommend..."
   "I actually think you should go with the larger package because..."
```

### 3.3 MEDDIC Qualification

```
M - Metrics: What are the quantifiable goals?
    "What KPI improvement are you targeting?"

E - Economic Buyer: Who has budget authority?
    "Who ultimately signs off on this investment?"

D - Decision Criteria: How will they decide?
    "What factors will determine which solution you choose?"

D - Decision Process: What are the steps?
    "Walk me through your typical procurement process."

I - Identify Pain: What is the critical business issue?
    "What happens if this problem isn't solved this quarter?"

C - Champion: Who is your internal advocate?
    "Who on your team is most invested in solving this?"
```

#### MEDDIC Scoring

| Element | 0 (None) | 1 (Partial) | 2 (Complete) |
|---------|----------|-------------|--------------|
| Metrics | Unknown | Vague targets | Specific, quantified |
| Economic Buyer | Unknown | Identified | Engaged & positive |
| Decision Criteria | Unknown | Partially known | Fully mapped |
| Decision Process | Unknown | High-level | Step-by-step with timeline |
| Identify Pain | Unknown | Surface-level | Deep, quantified impact |
| Champion | None | Passive supporter | Active advocate |

**Score 0-4**: Early stage, needs work
**Score 5-8**: Developing, invest carefully
**Score 9-12**: Strong opportunity, push forward

### 3.4 Sandler Selling System

```
The Sandler Submarine:

1. Bonding & Rapport → Build trust
2. Up-Front Contract → Set expectations for the meeting
3. Pain → Discover (3 levels: surface, business impact, personal impact)
4. Budget → Qualify financial fit
5. Decision → Map the decision process
6. Fulfillment → Present solution (only if qualified)
7. Post-Sell → Prevent buyer's remorse

Key Rule: "No mutual mystification"
Both sides should know exactly where they stand at all times.
```

## 4. SaaS Metrics & Unit Economics

### 4.1 Core SaaS Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Benchmark | Action if Bad |
|--------|---------|-----------|---------------|
| **MRR** | Sum of all monthly subscriptions | Growing >10% MoM (early) | Improve acquisition or reduce churn |
| **ARR** | MRR × 12 | >$1M for Series A | Focus on larger deals |
| **CAC** | Total S&M spend / New customers | < 1/3 of LTV | Optimize channels, improve conversion |
| **LTV** | ARPU × Gross Margin / Churn Rate | > 3× CAC | Increase ARPU, reduce churn, upsell |
| **Churn Rate** | Lost customers / Total customers | < 5% annually (enterprise) | Improve onboarding, support, product |
| **NRR** | (MRR + expansion - contraction - churn) / MRR | > 120% (best-in-class) | Focus on upsell/cross-sell |
| **Payback Period** | CAC / (ARPU × Gross Margin) | < 12 months | Lower CAC or increase ARPU |
| **Gross Margin** | (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue | > 70% for SaaS | Optimize infrastructure, support costs |

### 4.2 The SaaS Magic Number

```
Magic Number = Net New ARR / S&M Spend (previous quarter)

Interpretation:
< 0.5  → Inefficient, fix before scaling
0.5-0.75 → Acceptable, optimize
0.75-1.0 → Good, consider scaling
> 1.0  → Excellent, invest aggressively
```

### 4.3 Cohort Analysis Framework

```
Month    M0    M1    M2    M3    M4    M5    M6    M12
Jan      100%  85%   78%   74%   72%   70%   69%   65%
Feb      100%  88%   82%   78%   75%   73%   71%   -
Mar      100%  90%   84%   80%   77%   75%   -     -

What to look for:
- Flatten curve = good retention
- Steeper drop each month = worsening problem
- Improvement across cohorts = product is getting better
- "Smile curve" (uptick after dip) = expansion revenue
```

### 4.4 Revenue Breakdown

```
MRR Movements:

New MRR          + $50,000   ← New customers
Expansion MRR    + $20,000   ← Upsells, upgrades
Contraction MRR  - $5,000    ← Downgrades
Churn MRR        - $10,000   ← Lost customers
Reactivation MRR + $3,000    ← Win-backs
────────────────────────────
Net New MRR      = $58,000

Quick Ratio = (New + Expansion + Reactivation) / (Churn + Contraction)
            = $73,000 / $15,000
            = 4.87 (excellent, >4 is strong growth)
```

## 5. Pricing Psychology

### 5.1 Patrick Campbell - Price Intelligently

#### Pricing Strategy Framework

```
Step 1: Determine your pricing axis
├── Seat-based: Per user pricing
├── Usage-based: Per API call, per GB, per transaction
├── Feature-based: Tiers with different features
├── Outcome-based: Per lead generated, per conversion
└── Hybrid: Combination of above

Step 2: Find willingness to pay
├── Van Westendorp Price Sensitivity Meter
│   - At what price is this too expensive?
│   - At what price is this getting expensive but still acceptable?
│   - At what price is this a bargain?
│   - At what price is this too cheap to trust?
└── Conjoint Analysis (for complex products)

Step 3: Create value-based tiers
├── Free / Freemium (acquisition)
├── Starter (individual / small team)
├── Professional (growing team)
├── Enterprise (large org / custom)
```

#### Pricing Psychology Tactics

| Tactic | Description | Example |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| **Anchoring** | Show expensive option first | Enterprise ($999) → Pro ($199) → Starter ($49) |
| **Charm Pricing** | End in 7 or 9 | $97 instead of $100 |
| **Decoy Effect** | Add option to make target attractive | Small $5, Medium $8, Large $8.50 |
| **Price Framing** | Break into daily/weekly cost | "$3.27/day" instead of "$99/month" |
| **Bundle Pricing** | Package items together at discount | "Save 40% with the complete bundle" |
| **Tiered Value** | 3-4 tiers, highlight middle one | "Most Popular" badge on recommended tier |

### 5.2 Pricing Page Best Practices

```
Layout:
1. Three tiers (Good-Better-Best)
2. Highlight the recommended tier
3. Show annual savings vs monthly
4. Include "Most Popular" or "Best Value" badge
5. Social proof under each tier
6. Feature comparison table below
7. FAQ section addressing price objections
8. Money-back guarantee badge

Price Display:
- Show annual price per month: "$49/mo billed annually"
- Cross out the monthly price: "~~$69~~ $49/mo"
- Toggle for monthly/annual with savings shown
- Enterprise = "Contact Us" (never show price)
```

## 6. Funnel Architecture

### 6.1 Russell Brunson's Value Ladder

```
                                    ┌─────────────┐
                                    │  High-Ticket │  $5,000+
                                    │  1:1 / DFY   │  (Mastermind, consulting)
                                  ┌─┴─────────────┴─┐
                                  │   Core Offer      │  $500-$2,000
                                  │   (Course, SaaS)  │  (Main product)
                                ┌─┴───────────────────┴─┐
                                │   Low-Ticket Offer      │  $7-$97
                                │   (Mini-course, template)│  (Self-liquidating)
                              ┌─┴─────────────────────────┴─┐
                              │   Lead Magnet (FREE)          │  $0
                              │   (PDF, webinar, tool, quiz)  │  (Email capture)
                              └───────────────────────────────┘

Principle: Each step provides value AND creates desire for the next level.
```

### 6.2 TSL Funnel (Lucas Visky Model)

```
Traffic → Squeeze Page → Thank You Page → Sales Letter

Components:
1. Squeeze Page: Single opt-in, one promise, one CTA
2. Thank You Page: Deliver lead magnet + offer low-ticket
3. Sales Letter: Long-form page selling main offer
4. Order Bump: Add-on at checkout (+30% revenue)
5. Upsell 1: Complementary product after purchase
6. Upsell 2: High-ticket or continuity offer

Metrics to Track:
- Opt-in rate: Target 30-50%
- VSL watch rate: Target 30%+ to CTA
- Sales conversion: Target 2-5% of visitors
- Order bump take rate: Target 30-40%
- Upsell 1 conversion: Target 15-25%
- Upsell 2 conversion: Target 5-10%
```

### 6.3 Webinar Funnel

```
Registration Page → Confirmation → Webinar → Offer → Follow-up

Webinar Structure (90 min):
├── 0-5 min: Hook + Credibility
├── 5-15 min: Story (Origin → Transformation)
├── 15-60 min: Content (3 Secrets Framework)
│   ├── Secret 1: Reframe the vehicle
│   ├── Secret 2: Internal belief shift
│   └── Secret 3: External belief shift
├── 60-75 min: Transition to Offer (Stack Slide)
├── 75-85 min: Close + Urgency
└── 85-90 min: Q&A + Handle Objections

Key Metric: Show-up rate × Conversion rate × Price = Revenue per registration
```

## 7. Objection Handling

### 7.1 The LAER Framework

```
L - Listen (fully, without interrupting)
A - Acknowledge (validate the concern)
E - Explore (understand the root cause)
R - Respond (with a tailored answer)
```

### 7.2 Common Objections & Responses

#### "It's too expensive"

```
Response Framework:
1. Label: "It sounds like budget is a concern."
2. Explore: "Help me understand - too expensive compared to what?"
3. Reframe: "What's the cost of NOT solving this problem?"
4. Value stack: "When you consider that you get [X, Y, Z], the value is..."
5. Alternative: "We could start with [smaller package] and scale up."

Hormozi approach:
"If I could show you how this pays for itself in 30 days, would the
price still be a concern?"
```

#### "I need to think about it"

```
Response Framework:
1. Acknowledge: "I totally understand. This is an important decision."
2. Isolate: "When you say think about it, is it the price, the fit, or something else?"
3. Address: [Handle the real objection]
4. Urgency: "The only risk I see is that [problem continues/opportunity passes]"

Voss approach:
"It seems like there's something holding you back that we haven't addressed."
[Pause and let them fill the silence]
```

#### "We're already using [competitor]"

```
Response Framework:
1. Acknowledge: "[Competitor] is a solid product."
2. Explore: "What do you love most about them? What would you change?"
3. Differentiate: "Where we differ is [unique value prop]."
4. Risk reversal: "We offer a free pilot alongside your current solution."

Challenger approach:
"Most companies using [competitor] see great results with [specific feature].
But they often miss out on [capability we uniquely have], which costs them
an average of $X per month in [lost revenue/extra effort]."
```

#### "I don't have authority to decide"

```
Response Framework:
1. Validate: "I appreciate your honesty about the process."
2. Map: "Who else would need to be involved in this decision?"
3. Coach: "What would resonate most with them?"
4. Support: "Would it help if I prepared a summary you could share?"
5. Meet: "Would it make sense for me to join that conversation?"
```

#### "Now is not a good time"

```
Response Framework:
1. Empathize: "I completely understand. Timing matters."
2. Explore: "What would need to change for this to become a priority?"
3. Cost of delay: "Every month you wait, the problem costs approximately $X."
4. Low commitment: "Could we schedule a 15-minute check-in in [timeframe]?"
```

## 8. Sales Process & Pipeline

### 8.1 Sales Pipeline Stages

| Stage | Definition | Exit Criteria | Probability |
|-------|------------|---------------|-------------|
| **Prospecting** | Identifying potential customers | Confirmed fit with ICP | 10% |
| **Discovery** | Understanding needs and pains | SPIN/MEDDIC qualified | 20% |
| **Demo/Proposal** | Presenting solution | Stakeholder buy-in | 40% |
| **Negotiation** | Terms and pricing discussion | Agreement on terms | 60% |
| **Verbal Commit** | Handshake deal | Decision maker says yes | 80% |
| **Closed Won** | Signed contract, payment | Revenue booked | 100% |
| **Closed Lost** | Opportunity lost | Reason documented | 0% |

### 8.2 Pipeline Health Metrics

```
Pipeline Coverage Ratio = Pipeline Value / Quota
├── < 2x → Danger: not enough pipeline
├── 2-3x → Acceptable: monitor closely
├── 3-4x → Healthy: good coverage
└── > 5x → Bloated: clean up stale deals

Pipeline Velocity = (# Opportunities × Avg Deal Size × Win Rate) / Sales Cycle Length

Win Rate by Stage:
- Track conversion between each stage
- Identify where deals stall or die
- Focus training on weakest transition
```

## 9. Decision Frameworks

### 9.1 Deal Qualification Matrix

```
Should we pursue this deal?

Budget Confirmed?
├── NO → Can we help them build a business case? If no → Disqualify
└── YES ↓

Authority Identified?
├── NO → Can we find/develop a champion? If no → Disqualify
└── YES ↓

Need Confirmed?
├── NO → Can we create awareness of the need? If no → Disqualify
└── YES ↓

Timeline Defined?
├── NO → Is there a compelling event? If no → Nurture (not active pursuit)
└── YES → PURSUE ACTIVELY
```

### 9.2 Pricing Decision Framework

```
How to set the price:

1. Calculate value delivered to customer
2. Price at 10-20% of value delivered
3. Test with 10 prospects
4. If >50% say "yes" immediately → Price is too low
5. If <10% say "yes" → Price too high or wrong audience
6. If 20-30% say "yes" after negotiation → Price is right

Discounting Rules:
- Never discount without removing something
- Maximum discount: 20% (for annual prepay)
- No one-off discounts (creates precedent)
- Discount in exchange for case study/referral/multi-year
```

### 9.3 Channel Strategy Decision

```
Direct vs Channel vs PLG (Product-Led Growth):

ACV < $1,000 → PLG (self-serve, free trial)
ACV $1,000-$10,000 → Inside Sales + PLG
ACV $10,000-$50,000 → Inside Sales
ACV $50,000-$250,000 → Field Sales
ACV > $250,000 → Enterprise Sales + Partners

Note: ACV = Annual Contract Value
These are guidelines, not rules.
```

- [ ] Identified problems and pains (SPIN: Problem)

- [ ] Explored ideal outcome (SPIN: Need-Payoff)
- [ ] Identified decision maker and process (MEDDIC)
- [ ] Confirmed budget range
- [ ] Scheduled clear next step with date/time
- [ ] Sent follow-up email within 2 hours

### 10.2 Proposal Checklist

- [ ] Executive summary (their problem, our solution)
- [ ] Specific outcomes and metrics (not features)
- [ ] Social proof (relevant case studies)
- [ ] Investment options (3 tiers recommended)
- [ ] Timeline and milestones
- [ ] Team and expertise
- [ ] Guarantee or risk reversal
- [ ] Clear next steps and CTA
- [ ] Sent proposal live (not via email alone)

### 10.3 Lost Deal Analysis

- [ ] When did we lose (which stage)?
- [ ] Why did we lose (their stated reason)?
- [ ] Real reason (our assessment)?
- [ ] Who did they choose instead?
- [ ] What could we have done differently?
- [ ] Was this a qualified opportunity? (Re-evaluate ICP)
- [ ] Lessons captured and shared with team?
- [ ] Follow-up scheduled in 6 months?

### 10.4 Monthly Sales Health Check

- [ ] Pipeline coverage > 3x quota
- [ ] Win rate trending up or stable
- [ ] Average deal size stable or growing
- [ ] Sales cycle length stable or decreasing
- [ ] CAC within target range
- [ ] No single deal > 30% of pipeline
- [ ] All deals have next steps scheduled
- [ ] Lost deal reasons analyzed
- [ ] Forecast accuracy > 80%
